Hi, I’m Angie, a mixed-methods researcher passionate about understanding human psychology and making a meaningful impact to enhance human experiences

My journey as a researcher began at the the University of Sheffield pursuing the question of whether the universal grammar algorithm humans use to process language could be defined. This passion deepened at the University of Cambridge (MPhil) , where I conducted experimental linguistics research to examine how specific languages encode and communicate universal semantic information.

My eight years of professional experience as a researcher have been centered at the National Foreign Language Center (NFLC) where I have honed my expertise in program evaluation research from its inception, collecting, cleaning, and analyzing data to uncover variables that lead to a successful program. I led a $1M Department of Defense grant and authored six comprehensive evaluation reports for STARTLAK, a federally funded initiative that supports the teaching and learning of critical languages essential to national security. I synthesized data from over 3,000 responses per dataset to enhance user satisfaction and program outcomes. My ability to translate complex data into actionable recommendations has driven tangible results, including increased enrollment, improved instructional quality, and greater student and client satisfaction through the delivery of high-quality programs.

Currently I am focusing on completing my PhD at the University of Maryland, with a dissertation titled Why Do Learners’ Experiences Vary Within the Same Program? The Moderating Effects of Individual Differences on Satisfaction and Continued Motivation in Language Program Evaluation.

Throughout my academic and professional journey, I’ve refined my expertise in mixed-methods research, always with a goal of enhancing people’s experiences. I’m excited to apply these skills beyond the education sector, transitioning into UX research to design user-focused solutions that resonate deeply with people, enhance their interactions, and deliver meaningful, lasting impact.
